About PubMed Watcher

I am developing PubMed Watcher during my free time. This page should answer any questions you have about this application. If there are further things you would like to know or if want to provide some feedback, send me an email.

An open source project

The code behind PubMed watcher is freely available on GitHub under an Apache License 2.0. Submit pull requests if you think a feature is missing or something could be improved. The application has been built with the awesome Play! framework and relies on Twitter Bootstrap's delicious style for the front-end.

Based on data from PubMed

All the data displayed by PubMed Watcher comes straight from the neat PubMed-API. It guaranties fresh and up-to-date results. The articles present in the feed are based on a similarity metrics provided by PubMed: This score is calculated by looking at the amount of words shared by a pair articles. Basically, the higher the score is, the more related to articles are. You can read the paper describing the methodology if you want to know more about it. So in summary, each one of your Key Article has a list of related articles with a score attached to each of them.

With a pinch of heuristics

Now the idea behind PubMed Watcher is to pool the feeds coming from each one of your Key Article. If an article is present in more than one feed, it means that this article seems to be even more interesting to you, that's the heuristic. The redundant article then gets a new higher score which is the sum of all its indivual scores. Example, let's say you have two Key Articles named A and B. A has two similar articles F and G with respective similarity scores of 4 and 2. The Key Article B has two similar articles too: M and G with scores 7 and 6. The feed presented to you by PubMed Watcher will then be: G first (score of 6+2=8), M (score of 7) and finally F (4). This score is standardised in percentages (relative relatedness, the blue bars in the application), so here we would get: G (100%), M (88%) and F (50%). This metrics is not perfect yet it's intuitive and gives good enough results; plus it's fast to compute.

To simplify your life

You should come to PubMed Watcher every now and then, when you have time to read something. Your email address is used only to retrieve your profile.
